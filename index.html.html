LDR Sensor Project
An LDR (Light Dependent Resistor), also known as a photoresistor,
is a type of resistor whose resistance decreases with increasing
incident light intensity.
Applications:
Light Sensing: Used in automatic lighting systems, streetlights, and
camera light meters to detect ambient light levels.
Solar Panels: Used to detect light levels for optimizing solar panel
orientation and performance.
Alarms and Detectors: Can be used in burglar alarms to sense light
changes when doors or windows are opened.
Consumer Electronics: Found in gadgets like cameras and clocks for
automatic adjustment of brightness.
Radar Project Using Ultrasonic Sensor & Servo Motor
Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves above the human
hearing range (typically 40 kHz or higher) to determine
distances to objects and measure their proximity.
Applications:
Distance Measurement: Used in robotics and automation for obstacle
avoidance and navigation.
Presence Detection: Detecting the presence or absence of objects in
automated assembly lines or parking systems.
Level Measurement: Measuring liquid levels in tanks and containers.
Flow Rate Measurement: Monitoring flow rates of liquids and granular
materials.
